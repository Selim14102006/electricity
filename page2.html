  1. What are the components of electric power?

    Electric power components include:

    • Voltage (V): The potential difference that drives current.
    • Current (I): The flow of electric charges.
    • Power (P): The rate at which electrical energy is transferred by an electric circuit. It is calculated by P = V × I.




  2. Where is the unit of measurement (kilowatt) found?

    The kilowatt (kW) is a unit of power used to measure the rate of energy conversion or transfer. It is commonly used to express the output power of engines, electric motors, and the power consumption of appliances.

  3. What are the quantities in electrical engineering that change over time?

    The time-varying quantities in electrical engineering are:

    • Voltage (in AC circuits, voltage changes direction and magnitude over time)
    • Current (also varies in AC circuits)
    • Power (especially in AC systems where it fluctuates with voltage and current)

  4. What are the simple electrical circuits?

    Simple electrical circuits consist of:

    • Power Source (like a battery)
    • Conductor (wires)
    • Conductor (wires)
    • Switch (to open or close the circuit)




  5. What are the main electrical classifications? (Voltage classifications)

    The main electrical classifications based on voltage are:

    • Low Voltage (LV): Up to 1000V AC or 1500V DC
    • Medium Voltage (MV): From 1kV to 35kV
    • High Voltage (HV): From 35kV to 230kV
    • Extra High Voltage (EHV) : From 230kV upwards
